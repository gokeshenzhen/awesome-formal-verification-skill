Start with:

1. `cycle_swarm` at the lowest undetermined bound.
2. `bound_swarm` across a bounded deeper range.
3. `hunt -auto` for path/state diversity.

```tcl
hunt -config -strategy CS -mode cycle_swarm \
  -first_trace_attempt <bound> -engine_mode B -max_jobs <n>
hunt -run -strategy CS -property {<targets>}

hunt -config -strategy BS -mode bound_swarm \
  -first_trace_attempt <bound> \
  -max_trace_length <bound_plus_range> \
  -trace_attempt_time_limit 1s \
  -trace_attempt_time_limit_factor 10
hunt -run -strategy BS -property {<targets>}

hunt -run -auto -property {<targets>} -time_limit <time>
```

Use `state_swarm` when useful milestone covers exist, `trace_swarm` when valuable traces exist, and `loop_swarm` for liveness CEX.

A no-hit run means only: no witness was found under that mode, seed, and resource budget. Preserve the result as `undetermined`; it is not proof, unreachability, bounded signoff, or exhaustive bug absence. AUTO also may not cross the existing proof bound—inspect helper depths.
